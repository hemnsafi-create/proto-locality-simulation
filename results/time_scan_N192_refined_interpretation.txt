Refined time-scan result (N=192)

Run:
- alpha = [1.74, 1.80]
- T = [320, 330, 340, 350, 360]

- beta = gamma = 1
- dt = 0.01
- sample_every = 10

Main finding:
For N=192, clustering onset depends on alpha and occurs earlier for larger alpha.

Detailed interpretation:
- alpha = 1.74:
  onset begins between T=330 and T=340
  robust clustering by about T=350

- alpha = 1.80:
  onset begins between T=320 and T=330
  robust clustering by about T=340

Interpretation for the paper:
This refined scan strengthens the conclusion that time-to-cluster grows strongly with system size N.
It also shows that, at fixed N=192, increasing alpha from 1.74 to 1.80 advances the onset time by roughly 10-20 time units.

Suggested wording:
For N=192, the clustering onset is not fixed at a single time but depends on alpha: it appears around Tİ330 for alpha=1.80 and around Tİ340 for alpha=1.74, becoming clearly robust by Tİ340-350.
