To assess sensitivity to the initialization protocol, we analyzed a sweep comprising 270 runs across two protocol choices (absolute and relative), two coupling values (alpha = 1.74, 1.8), and three system sizes (N = 64, 128, 256). Aggregation over the cell-level summaries shows that the final lambda2 means are numerically zero in all cells after standard numerical-zero handling. For the final chi_hhi statistic, the relative protocol yields a larger mean-than-absolute value in 5 of the 6 (alpha, N) comparisons, with the largest positive difference occurring at alpha = 1.8, N = 256 (relative - absolute = +0.009883). A single comparison is negative, at alpha = 1.74, N = 256 (relative - absolute = -0.001294). Overall, the protocol dependence of final chi_hhi is small in magnitude across this sweep, while the sign of the effect is predominantly favorable to the relative initialization.
